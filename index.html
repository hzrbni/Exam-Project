  • Not only planets spin - the Sun completes one rotation around it`s axis in 25 days.

What it is, how it works, and how the energy gets to us here on Earth..

Things to Know About Sun

As the center of our Solar system, the Sun keeps all 8 planets moving around it. It let`s out incredible masses of energy all around it and keeps our Earth healthy, beautiful and most importantly comfortable for us to live on! Because of it plants grow and people get their needed vitamin D that keeps us healthy. Sun is needed for us to survive but a little too much of it`s power can destroy everything!

Mercury
  • Temperature –183c / +427c
  • Size 2440 km
  • Terrain craters
  • Distance from Sun 57 910 000 km
  • One day on Earth 58 days
  • Gravity 3,7 m/s²

Planet Description

As the closest planet to the Sun, the view from Mercury would be fascinating! Sun would appear 3 times larger than how we see it on Earth. A day on this planet would be the longest you`ve ever experienced - 59 Earth days, hovever, it wouldn`t be a pleasant day and the night wouldn`t be any better because the temperatures range from melting hot to freezing cold!

Planet Facts

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar system.
  • It`s named after Roman messenger of the Gods, because it orbits incredibly fast!
  • Mercury`s weather never changes and there are no seasons.
  • It has only 38% of Earth`s gravity so you would be much lighter there!
Venus
  • Temperature +462 °C
  • Size 6052 km
  • Terrain volcanoes
  • Distance from Sun 108 200 000 km
  • One day on Earth equals 116 days
  • Gravity 3,7 m/s²

Planet Description

Even though this planet is about the same size and mass as our own, it is very different. It spins incredibly slowly so one day on Venus lasts roughly a year on Earth! It`s not just a flat boring rock in the sky, it has many volcanos and mountains as well as flat areas. If the fact that it`s the hottest planet doesn`t impress you, there are also mind-blowingly fast winds that carry the clouds around the whole planet in only 5 Earth days.

Planet Facts

  • Some people call it the “morning star” and “evening star”. It`s so bright that it`s the brightes object in our sky right after Moon!
  • Venus rotates in the opposit direction to other planets.
  • It`s the hottest planet in our Solar system!
  • The atmosphere on Venus is 92 times stronger than on Earth.

It has the widest range of animals, birds, bugs and other creatures - explorers and scientists still manage to find new species quite often. There is still a lot to discover!

Mars
  • Temperature −63 °C
  • Size 3396 km
  • Terrain stone and dust
  • Distance from Sun 227 900 000 km
  • One day on Earth 1 day
  • Gravity 3,711 m/s²

A day on Mars wouldn`t be much longer than a day on Earth but the experience would definitely be different! Mars is a cold, dusty desert planet. There is strong evidence that there used to be water there and that it used to be much warmer but now the weather and air has changed drastically and the strongest forces there are the huge dust storms.

  • It`s the second smallest planet right after Mercury.
  • Pieces of Mars have been found on Earth!
  • It has the highest mountain in the Solar system - 21 km tall.
  • The largest dust storms appear frequently on Mars.
Jupiter
  • Temperature −130 °C
  • Size 69 911 km
  • Terrain liquid
  • Distance from Sun 778 500 000 km
  • One day on Earth 10 hours
  • Gravity 24,79 m/s²

Planet Description

On Jupiter a day lasts less than a half of a day on Earth. You would think that years would pass like seasons there but actually a year on our planet lasts 12 years on Jupiter! It`s measured by the time it completes a whole circle (orbit) around the Sun.

Planet Facts

  • The largest and most massive planet in our Solar system!
  • Jupiter also has a ring system around it, hovever, not as noticable as Saturn.
  • There is a huge red spot on the planet which is a storm that has been active for over 300 years.
  • There is no solid ground on Jupiter, only thick liquid hydrogen layer.
Saturn
  • Temperature −175 °C
  • Size 58 232 km
  • Terrain hydrogen and helium gases
  • Distance from Sun 1,434E9 km
  • One day on Earth 10h
  • Gravity 10,44 m/s²

Planet Description

It`s curious that only Saturn is usually shown with a ring system. In reality all outer planets have rings around them. For some planets they`re just not that easy to notice. Did you know, that every 29 years there is an optical illusion and it looks like the planet doesn`t even have the famous rings? It`s because the planet and it`s rings tilt a little and they appear as a thin line!

Planet Facts

  • It is possible to see this planet from Earth without a telescope!
  • Saurn is known for it`s ring system. The rings strech as far as 12,700 km from the planet hovever they are only 20 meters thick!
  • The fastest winds can be found on Saturn`s surface - as strong as 1,800 km per hour.
  • This planet`s moons are feozen and on some of them there are oceans hiding under the ice.
Uranus
  • Temperature −224 °C
  • Size 25 362 km
  • Terrain ice and gas
  • Distance from Sun 2,871E9 km
  • One day on Earth 17h
  • Gravity 8,87 m/s²

Planet Description

It`s sometimes called the weirdest planet in our Solar system and not only because it`s the only one rotating on it`s side but also because of it`s characteristics. It looks boring and plane but it`s made out of a freezing icy liquid mix and it`s clouds are considered to be smelly because they have hydrogen sulfide gas in them which stinks just like rotten eggs!

Planet Facts

  • Just like Venus, Uranus turns the opposit direction of other planets.
  • Uranus` axis is so tilted that the planet is on it`s side!
  • This planet also has rings! It is known at the moment that there are at least 13 of them.
  • It is the coldest planet in the Solar system and also has strong winds.
Neptune
  • Temperature -214 Celsius
  • Size 24 622 km
  • Terrain gas and ice
  • Distance from Sun 4,495E9 km
  • One day on Earth 58 days
  • Gravity 11,15 m/s²

Planet Description

Neptune is cold, dark and incredibly windy. In fact, the planet is so dark and distant from us that it`s the only planet we can`t see in the night sky. If it`s climate doesn`t sound rough enough, it`s clouds are masses of frozen gas and they get blown with the force of the wind of 2,000 km/h - it`s close to the maximum speed of a high-speed fighter jet. Imagine being in a storm like that!

Planet Facts

  • Like Jupiter, Neptune has a giant storm described as the Great Dark spot and is as big as Earth!
  • It has another smaller storm and is called the Small Dark spot. It`s as big as our Moon.
  • The planet has active storms and strong winds on it`s surface.
  • Neptune has a ring system, all though not very visible and mainly made from ice and dust.
