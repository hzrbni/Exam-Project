Planet Description

As the closest planet to the Sun, the view from Mercury would be fascinating! Sun would appear 3 times larger than how we see it on Earth. A day on this planet would be the longest you`ve ever experienced - 59 Earth days, hovever, it wouldn`t be a pleasant day and the night wouldn`t be any better because the temperatures range from melting hot to freezing cold!

Planet Facts

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in the Solar system.
  • It`s named after Roman messenger of the Gods, because it orbits incredibly fast!
  • Mercury`s weather never changes and there are no seasons.
  • It has only 38% of Earth`s gravity so you would be much lighter there!
Venus
  • Temperature +462 °C
  • Size 6052 km
  • Terrain volcanoes
  • Distance from Sun 108 200 000 km
  • One day on Earth equals 116 days
  • Gravity 3,7 m/s²

Planet Description

Even though this planet is about the same size and mass as our own, it is very different. It spins incredibly slowly so one day on Venus lasts roughly a year on Earth! It`s not just a flat boring rock in the sky, it has many volcanos and mountains as well as flat areas. If the fact that it`s the hottest planet doesn`t impress you, there are also mind-blowingly fast winds that carry the clouds around the whole planet in only 5 Earth days.

Planet Facts

  • Some people call it the “morning star” and “evening star”. It`s so bright that it`s the brightes object in our sky right after Moon!
  • Venus rotates in the opposit direction to other planets.
  • It`s the hottest planet in our Solar system!
  • The atmosphere on Venus is 92 times stronger than on Earth.

Planet Description

By now you have experienced living on this planet and you know how fun the days are and how beautiful the night sky is. But what else could you learn? Well, it is curious that 70% of our planet is covered in water and we have oceans so deep that we have still not reached the bottom. In the depth there are creatures that look alien-like! Also on our extremely diverse terrain filled with deserts, volcanos, forests and mountains, there is jungle. It has the widest range of animals, birds, bugs and other creatures - explorers and scientists still manage to find new species quite often. There is still a lot to discover!

Mars
  • Temperature −63 °C
  • Size 3396 km
  • Terrain stone and dust
  • Distance from Sun 227 900 000 km
  • One day on Earth 1 day
  • Gravity 3,711 m/s²

A day on Mars wouldn`t be much longer than a day on Earth but the experience would definitely be different! Mars is a cold, dusty desert planet. There is strong evidence that there used to be water there and that it used to be much warmer but now the weather and air has changed drastically and the strongest forces there are the huge dust storms.

  • It`s the second smallest planet right after Mercury.
  • Pieces of Mars have been found on Earth!
  • It has the highest mountain in the Solar system - 21 km tall.
  • The largest dust storms appear frequently on Mars.
Jupiter
  • Temperature −130 °C
  • Size 69 911 km
  • Terrain liquid
  • Distance from Sun 778 500 000 km
  • One day on Earth 10 hours
  • Gravity 24,79 m/s²

Planet Description

On Jupiter a day lasts less than a half of a day on Earth. You would think that years would pass like seasons there but actually a year on our planet lasts 12 years on Jupiter! It`s measured by the time it completes a whole circle (orbit) around the Sun.

Planet Facts

  • The largest and most massive planet in our Solar system!
  • Jupiter also has a ring system around it, hovever, not as noticable as Saturn.
  • There is a huge red spot on the planet which is a storm that has been active for over 300 years.
  • There is no solid ground on Jupiter, only thick liquid hydrogen layer.
